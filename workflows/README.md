# ⚙️ Workflows  
### Practical ways to integrate AI into teaching and academic work

---

## 🌟 Overview

This section contains **step-by-step workflows** for using AI tools to support teaching, learning, and academic tasks.

Workflows are designed to be:
- Practical and repeatable  
- Adaptable across tools and contexts  
- Grounded in human-in-the-loop decision making  

---

## 🧠 What Is a Workflow?

A workflow is a structured process for completing a task using AI.

Instead of:
- Asking one question and accepting the output  

Workflows focus on:
- Defining the task  
- Providing context  
- Guiding AI interaction  
- Evaluating and refining results  

---

## 🔁 Core Idea

All workflows are grounded in this process:

**Observe → Think → Act → Reflect**

- **Observe** → Understand the problem  
- **Think** → Define context and constraints  
- **Act** → Use AI to generate output  
- **Reflect** → Evaluate and improve  

👉 The goal is not just to get an answer—it’s to improve thinking and outcomes.

## 🎯 Purpose

These workflows are designed to help:

- Faculty save time while maintaining quality  
- Students engage in meaningful, structured learning  
- Educators integrate AI intentionally—not reactively  

---

## 👀 Human in the Loop

Every workflow emphasizes the role of the human.

AI can:
- Generate  
- Suggest  
- Assist  

But the human must:
- Interpret  
- Evaluate  
- Decide  

> AI increases capability—but also increases responsibility.

---

## 🚀 How to Use These Workflows

You can:
- Follow workflows step-by-step  
- Adapt them to your own teaching context  
- Modify prompts and structure as needed  
- Combine workflows for more complex tasks  

---

## 🌱 Philosophy

AI does not replace thinking—it redistributes it.

This section reflects an approach to teaching that prioritizes:
- Structured problem solving  
- Intentional AI use  
- Critical evaluation  
- Continuous improvement
