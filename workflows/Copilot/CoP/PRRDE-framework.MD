# 🧠 PRRDE Framework  
### Prompt → Review → Refine → Decide → Execute  
**Responsible Generative AI Workflow (Copilot)**

---

## 🌟 Overview

This workflow provides a structured, repeatable framework for using generative AI responsibly in professional and academic contexts.

It is designed to:
- Improve consistency and quality of AI-assisted work  
- Reduce risk (inaccuracy, misalignment, overreliance)  
- Reinforce human judgment and accountability  

Originally developed for faculty and staff, this framework is also adaptable for student learning.

---

## 🎯 Problem This Solves

As AI tools like Microsoft Copilot become embedded in daily work, users operate at different levels of experience.

Without structure, this can lead to:
- Unverified or inaccurate information  
- Misalignment with institutional policies  
- Overreliance on AI-generated content  
- Inconsistent communication quality  

👉 This workflow introduces a **clear, repeatable process** for responsible AI use.

---

## 🧩 The Workflow

### 🔁 Prompt → Review → Refine → Decide → Execute

This workflow follows a structured decision-making loop:

1. **Prompt** → Define the task clearly  
2. **Review** → Evaluate output critically  
3. **Refine** → Improve with targeted revisions  
4. **Decide** → Apply professional judgment  
5. **Execute** → Implement responsibly  

---

## 🧠 Step 1: Prompt (Define)

Use the **GCSE framework** to structure your prompt:

- **Goal** → What should AI do?  
- **Context** → Audience, constraints, situation  
- **Source** → Files or references to ground output  
- **Expectations** → Tone, format, length, inclusions  

### Example Prompt

> Draft a weekly course announcement for a 12-week course.  
>  
> Goal: Reinforce expectations and deadlines  
> Context: Undergraduate students, professional tone  
> Source: Syllabus and course schedule  
> Expectations: 3 short paragraphs, clear due dates, emphasize time management  

### Why This Matters

- Reduces hallucinations  
- Improves relevance  
- Anchors output in real sources  
- Minimizes rework later  

---

## 🔍 Step 2: Review (Evaluate)

Pause and critically evaluate the AI output.

Ask:

- Is this **accurate** based on sources?  
- Does it **align with policy and expectations**?  
- Is the **tone appropriate**?  
- Is anything **missing or unclear**?  

### Example Review Prompts

- “Identify unsupported claims in this response.”  
- “Check alignment with institutional policy.”  
- “Highlight vague or unclear language.”  

---

## ✏️ Step 3: Refine (Improve)

Make **targeted improvements** based on review.

Focus on:
- Clarity  
- Structure  
- Tone alignment  
- Removing vague language  
- Strengthening expectations  

### Example Refinement Prompts

- “Condense by 20% while preserving meaning.”  
- “Clarify expectations in paragraph two.”  
- “Adjust tone to professional and policy-aligned.”  

---

## ⚖️ Step 4: Decide (Human Judgment)

Before using the output, ask:

- Is this **factually correct**?  
- Does it reflect **my professional judgment**?  
- Would I **stand behind this** without mentioning AI?  
- Are there **risks or unintended implications**?  

👉 This is the most important step.  
AI informs. Humans decide.

---

## 🚀 Step 5: Execute (Responsible Use)

When finalizing:

- Confirm edits are complete  
- Ensure compliance with policies  
- Save in approved systems  
- Share appropriately (LMS, Teams, email, etc.)  

### Optional Transparency Statement

> “This document was developed with the assistance of Microsoft Copilot and reviewed for accuracy and alignment.”

---

## ⚠️ Data Responsibility

Before using AI:

- Do NOT include confidential or protected data (FERPA, HR, etc.)  
- Use approved institutional storage (SharePoint, OneDrive, Teams)  
- Follow data governance and privacy policies  

---

## ✅ Tips for Success

### DO:
- Define your goal clearly  
- Anchor in real documents  
- Use targeted refinement prompts  
- Treat AI as a drafting partner  

### DON’T:
- Publish without review  
- Assume AI is correct  
- Upload sensitive data  
- Replace professional judgment  

---

## 🧪 Classroom Adaptation

This framework can be adapted into student assignments.

Example: **AI Lab Report Structure**

Students document:
- Prompt (Define)  
- AI Output  
- Review & Verification  
- Refinement  
- Decision & Reflection  
- Transparency statement  

👉 This shifts assessment from output → thinking process.

---

## 🧠 Philosophy

AI is not a shortcut—it is a structured thinking partner.

This workflow emphasizes:
- Critical evaluation  
- Iterative improvement  
- Responsible decision-making  
- Human ownership of outcomes  

---

## 📊 Key Insight

> “AI output should be interrogated—not accepted.”
